I am interested in identifying physiological mechanisms of brain function, in particular, the neural substrates of flexible, goal-directed behavior. For this, I build neural network and reinforcement learning models. I am also very interested in meta-learning in the brain and in AI.

While keeping a strong interest in computational neuroscience, my new endeavors seek to adapt computationally efficient neural circuit mechanisms into machine learning architectures (such as deep neural networks) aiming for more flexible / powerful learning algorithms in AI and brain science.

I am currently an Associate Research Scientist at [Yale University](https://www.yale.edu). I am part of the [Decision Lab](https://www.levydecisionlab.com), led by Ifat Levy. Previously as a Postdoctoral Research Scientist, I collaborated with Nancy Kopell [(Boston University)](https://www.bu.edu), Thilo Womelsdorf [(Vanderbilt University)](https://www.vanderbilt.edu), and Xiao-Jing Wang [(New York University)](https://www.nyu.edu). I earned my PhD in Computational Neuroscience at the [Neuroscience Institute (UMH-CSIC)](http://in.umh-csic.es), under the supervision of Albert Compte [(IDIBAPS – Hospital Clinic)](https://www.clinicbarcelona.org/en/idibaps).
